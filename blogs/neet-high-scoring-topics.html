NEET 2026 High-Scoring Topics: Physics, Chemistry and Biology Guide

Focusing on high-scoring topics in NEET 2026 can boost your rank significantly, as these chapters account for 70-80% of questions across Physics (25% weightage), Chemistry (25%), and Biology (50%), based on past trends and NTA patterns. Prioritizing them ensures efficient prep for the 180-question, 720-mark exam, emphasizing NCERT concepts with application-based twists.

High-Scoring Physics Topics

Physics demands numerical mastery; target Mechanics and Electrodynamics for 40-50% coverage, as they frequently test laws, equations, and real-world applications.

  • Laws of Motion (7%), Work, Energy & Power (7%), System of Particles and Rotational Motion (7%), and Gravitation (7%), where problems on friction, collisions, and orbital mechanics dominate.
  • Electrostatics (10%) and Current Electricity (10%) are scoring via formula application in fields, circuits, and potential differences, often with diagram-based questions.
  • Thermodynamics and Thermal Properties (6-10%) cover laws, heat engines, and entropyâ€”practice derivations for accuracy.
  • Optics (Ray & Wave, 5-8%) and Modern Physics (Atoms/Nuclei/Semiconductors, 6-10%) yield quick scores through photoelectric effect and lens formulas.
  • Avoid low-weightage like Units/Measurements (4%) initially; revise formulas daily.

High-Scoring Chemistry Topics

Chemistry balances theory and calculations; Organic (34%) and Physical (32%) sections score high with NCERT reactions and equations.

  • In Organic, Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids (9%) lead with carbonyl mechanisms, followed by Hydrocarbons (7%) on alkanes/alkenes and Chemical Bonding (7%) on VSEPR/hybridization.
  • p-Block Elements (6-13%) in Inorganic cover group trends and compoundsâ€”memorize exceptions for easy points.
  • Equilibrium (6%) and Thermodynamics (5%) in Physical Chemistry test Le Chatelier's principle and enthalpyâ€”focus on graphs and numericals.
  • Coordination Compounds (5-6%) and Electrochemistry (5%) involve isomerism and Nernst equation; practice mole concepts for speed.
  • d/f-Block (6%) and Biomolecules (4%) are conceptualâ€”link to everyday applications for retention.
  • Prioritize NCERT examples over advanced books for 80% direct questions.

High-Scoring Biology Topics

Biology's 360 marks make it pivotal: Human Physiology (20%) tops with digestion, respiration, and neural controlâ€”master diagrams for 10-12 questions.

  • Genetics and Evolution (18%) covers Mendelian laws, mutations, and Hardy-Weinbergâ€”apply to pedigrees and disorders.
  • Ecology and Environment (12-16%) scores via ecosystems, biodiversity, and pollution cyclesâ€”NCERT flowcharts are key.
  • Reproduction (10%) includes human/plant processes and contraceptivesâ€”focus on diagrams and exceptions.
  • Plant Physiology (13%) emphasizes photosynthesis, transport, and hormonesâ€”link to experiments.
  • Biotechnology (6-7%) and Cell Structure (10%) test principles and cell cycleâ€”revise molecular basis.
  • Diversity in Living World (10%) and Human Health/Disease (9%) are memory-based; use tables for phyla and pathogens.
  • Revise all NCERT lines, as 90% questions are direct.

Preparation Strategy

  • Allocate 40% time to high-weightage chapters, starting with NCERT reading followed by PYQs and mocks for pattern recognition.
